Being ‘accessible’ is a fickle term, particularly when it comes to wheelchairs. The level of accessibility could fluctuate from a handicap parking space to a personal concierge depending on the location. Due to this, wheelchair userscan easily run into awkward situations where they can’t go somewhere dueonly to the fact that they have a wheelchair. That’s not fair, right? Right.

This is the very issue that whee// aims to address. Providing those who need it access to accessibility information for the location they want to go to before they go gives people a better quality of life, never having to guess at whether or not they will be able to enjoy themselves.
